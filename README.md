# 🏥 Clinic Management System

## 📌 Introduction
The **Clinic Management System** is a database-driven application designed to streamline the administrative and operational processes of a clinic. This system automates tasks such as **patient registration, doctor scheduling, inventory tracking, and billing**, enhancing efficiency, minimizing manual errors, and improving patient care. It serves as an integrated platform connecting **patients, doctors, receptionists, and laboratory staff** seamlessly.

---

## 🎯 Motivation
Traditional clinic operations often rely on **manual processes**, leading to **inefficiencies, errors, and delays**. With the increasing demand for **digital transformation in healthcare**, an automated system is essential to **enhance clinic operations**. The Clinic Management System aims to address these challenges by providing a **secure, reliable, and user-friendly platform** that helps clinics **manage patient loads efficiently, ensure data accuracy, and maintain service quality**, ultimately improving the overall **patient and staff experience**.

---

## 🏆 Objectives
- **Reliable Data Storage & Access:**  
  The system serves as a **centralized repository** for patient records, appointment schedules, billing information, and inventory details, ensuring **easy access** for authorized users.

- **Reduced Operational Costs:**  
  By automating **appointment scheduling, inventory management, and billing**, the system minimizes manual labor, reducing **errors and unnecessary expenses**.

- **Data Protection & Compliance:**  
  The system implements **encryption, role-based access control, and secure authentication** mechanisms.

---

## 📌 Scope of the Project
The system will cover the following key functionalities:

1. **Patient Management** – Registration, appointment booking, and medical history.
2. **Doctor Management** – Scheduling, specialization tracking, and patient interactions.
3. **Billing System** – Invoice generation and payment tracking.
4. **Inventory Management** – Supplies monitoring and procurement.
5. **Laboratory Management** – Test scheduling and report generation.
6. **Receptionist Role** – Handling appointments, patient records, and billing assistance.

---

## 🔧 Requirements

### **Functional Requirements**

### **Non-Functional Requirements**

---

## 📊 Entity Relationship Diagram (ERD)
The database consists of the following entities:

- **Patients** – Stores personal details, contact information, and medical history.
- **Doctors** – Tracks specialization, experience, and assigned patients.
- **Prescriptions** – Links doctors, patients, and medications.
- **Tests & Reports** – Records test details, results, and associated costs.
- **Inventory** – Manages medical supplies and procurement.
- **Billing** – Integrates costs of treatments, tests, and inventory usage.
- **Receptionists** – Manages appointments, billing, and clinic operations.
